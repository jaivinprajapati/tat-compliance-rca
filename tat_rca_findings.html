Problem Decomposition — Splitting the −7.3pp Drop

Why not split by Ticket Type (Service vs Others)?

It's the obvious first cut, but it's misleading. "Service" and "Others" are TAT-rule classifications, not workflow categories. Inside Service there are two very different sub-types behaving oppositely:

ISD's volume share grew (because overall volume dropped post-Holi while ISD stayed stable), creating a +3.6pp mix-shift gain that offset RDNI's −4.0pp damage. Aggregating them made Service look fine at −0.4pp — but RDNI was silently worse.

The correct cut: by resolution ownership (Px vs Cx)

Every ticket is routed to a queue based on who can actually fix it:

  • Px queue — requires a CSP/partner (infra, connectivity, physical)
  • Cx queue — resolved by Wiom customer service (backend, account, payments, app)

The queue assignment is near-perfect (sub-types are 99-100% one queue). This gives us two operationally distinct problems:

Three sub-types drive 87% of the Px drop — all connectivity/internet-quality issues. From the customer's perspective these are the same kind of problem: "my internet isn't working properly." From a TAT-rule perspective they're split across Service (RDNI) and Others (FD, SS), but they should be investigated together.

What to investigate

  • RDNI lifecycle: Why does "recharge done but internet not working" now take longer? Is it auto-created post-recharge? Check the recharge → activation → router sync flow for failure points.
  • Partner cohort effect: Earlier analysis showed small partners (20–99 Dec tickets) had the largest rate drop (−8.8pp). Is this a capacity/training issue concentrated in that segment?
  • Connectivity quality: Frequent Disconnection and Slow Speed are degraded-experience tickets (not hard outages). Cross-reference with router uptime / ping flap data from SQ-CSP dashboards.

This is not one broken workflow — nearly every Cx sub-type degraded 10–45pp. Top sub-type (Help in renewal) explains 57% of Cx drop, but the rest spreads across 10+ categories. Pattern signals a systemic issue at the operation level, not a specific workflow.

What to investigate

  • Capacity / Staffing: Did Cx team size change? Tickets-per-agent-per-day Dec vs now. Attrition or ramp-up of new hires?
  • System / Tool change: Was there a Kapture or backend change in Jan–Feb? Map the inflection date to release notes.
  • Crowd-out effect: Several sub-types grew 10–130× in volume (Unable to Pay via App: 5 → 663). New workflows entering without added capacity would push older workflows out of TAT.
  • Process changes: Was a new approval or verification step added for refunds, plan changes, account edits?
