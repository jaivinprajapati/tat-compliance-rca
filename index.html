Its rate collapsed from 93.6% to 79.0% (−14.6pp). Service dropped only 4.5pp and its weighted contribution barely changed. The problem is overwhelmingly in "Others" tickets.

What do these ticket types actually contain?

Service (9 sub-types, but effectively 2): 99.9% of Service tickets are infrastructure outages — "Internet Supply Down" (85%) and "Recharge done but internet not working" (15%). Clean, well-defined bucket. Always needs a technician on-site.

Others (109 sub-types): A true catch-all spanning completely unrelated workflows — non-outage internet issues (Frequent Disconnection, Slow Speed), payment/billing (Help in renewal, Autopay, refunds), change requests (name/plan/password updates), partner misbehavior complaints, app bugs, and a long tail of one-offs.

This is the first clue: "Others" isn't one problem. It's multiple distinct workflows sharing the same 24h TAT.

Within "Others" — Two Different Problems (Px vs Cx Queue)

Looking at the sub-types in "Others", an obvious question emerges: do all of these actually need a partner (CSP) to resolve? No. "Autopay issue" doesn't need a CSP. "Change name/mobile" doesn't need a CSP. But "Frequent Disconnection" does.

The queue assignment (Px vs Cx) is a near-perfect classifier:

  • Px queue — tickets routed to the partner/CSP (infra, physical issues)
  • Cx queue — tickets routed to Wiom customer service (backend, account, software)

The overall −7.3pp drop is almost evenly split between two completely different problems:
  • Problem A (53% — Partner side): Partner queue TAT went from 79.7% to 74.3% (−5.3pp). Already the weaker bucket; further degrading. CSP-driven, infrastructure-related, needs technicians on site.
  • Problem B (47% — Wiom side): Wiom customer service queue collapsed from 95.9% to 75.1% (−20.8pp). Was excellent, now mid-70s. Pure Wiom-owned workflows (payments, refunds, account changes). No CSP can help here.
Two owners, two root causes, two separate RCAs needed.

Next Steps

The drop is two separate problems with different owners. Next we need to diagnose each:

  • Wiom CS queue (Cx) RCA: Why did 95.9% TAT collapse to 75.1%? Lifecycle breakdown on the top Cx sub-types (Help in renewal, Autopay issue, Demand coupons, Refunds, Change requests). Focus on first-touch handling, reopens, and resolution-path changes.
  • Partner queue (Px) RCA: Why did 79.7% drop to 74.3%? Concentrate on Others-Px sub-types (Frequent Disconnection, Slow Speed, Router issues) since they degraded more than Service. Cross-cut with partner cohort (small partners driving most of it) and lifecycle (assignment gaps, reopens).
