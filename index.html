Finding: Inflection point — two-wave degradation

Cx breach rate was stable at ~2% from Dec through Jan 25. Then two clear breaks:

  • Jan 27 — first spike. Breach rate jumped from ~1.5% to 10.1% on a single day. 7-day avg began climbing.
  • Feb 4–6 — major break. Three consecutive days hit 15.8%, 14.2%, 32.1% breach rate. A second wave Feb 10–11 peaked at 25.9% and 30.8%. System never recovered — settled at 20–30% as the new normal.

Daily Cx breach rate & tickets created (Dec 01 – Mar 12)

Two discrete breaks, not a gradual drift. Jan 27 and Feb 4-6 are specific event dates. Sharp single-day jumps (1.7% → 10.1%, then 7.2% → 32.1%) suggest system/process changes on those dates, not capacity erosion. Daily Cx volume also roughly doubled in the same window (~250/day → ~500/day) — a major signal the Cx team should map against releases and policy changes.

10Hypothesis Check — Are Reopens Driving TAT Breaches?

Hypothesis: Reopen rate has risen (2.3% → 4.7% in Px, 0.9% → 7.8% in Cx). Since TAT is measured creation → final resolution, reopens mechanically extend resolution time and push tickets past TAT. Is the TAT breach increase being driven by more reopens?

Decomposition of breach rate increase

Px+5.0pp+3.7pp (73%)+1.3pp (27%)
Cx+21.6pp+18.7pp (87%)+2.9pp (13%)
Verdict: Hypothesis partially holds, but reopens are not the primary driver.
  • In both queues, non-reopened tickets getting worse accounts for 73% (Px) and 87% (Cx) of the breach increase.
  • Reopens contribute, but modestly — 27% in Px and 13% in Cx.
  • The reopen-vs-non-reopen breach gap stayed stable (~50pp). Reopens aren't suddenly more toxic — they were always toxic; they just happen more often now.
  • Reopens rising is a correlated symptom, likely sharing the same upstream cause as the underlying TAT degradation.
